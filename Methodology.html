Methodology

The goal of this project is to research and highlight the Nevada news desert, following a national trend of local media disappearing across the U.S., leaving communities without a voice. For this project, a news desert is best defined as a geographic zone with little to no access to media. The focus of this research is on the Spanish-speaking population in Nevada and how their vulnerabilities can be linked back to the lack of media providing information in their language.

Sources for this project were selected for either their professional experience working as a journalist or expertise in the topic of Latino climate vulnerabilities. Hilda Berganza is a climate manager with the Hispanic Access Foundation, and I had previously talked to her about Latino conservation and the importance of Latinos to care for and learn about the environment around them. In that interview, she told me about the Project she leads, El Aire Que Respiramos, which examined different cities in the U.S with high populations of Latinos and identified the hazards in the air. Leo Murrieta is a Director with Make the Road Nevada, a non-profit that helps Latinos navigate issues on topics such as Climate Justice. Make the Road Nevada specializes in advocacy and bringing about policy change in Nevada. Dunia Elvir is an anchor with Telemundo and the President of the National Association of Hispanic Journalists, which is a non-profit that is committed to the development, recognition, and representation of Hispanic journalists in the industry. Maria Palma is a reporter with KUNR who is the sole producer of Al Aire, a Spanish-language newscast that runs down the major stories of the station once a week. Emilio Milo is an intern at KUNR who is under the guidance of Maria and is learning how to do Bilingual reporting.

Nevada was selected as the location for this project as it’s the state where I am currently doing my studies and have begun my professional reporting. This is not to say that it's a problem impacting only Nevada. As mentioned in the project, the CUNY project is aware of the lack of representation that people in Oakland feel about being overshadowed by San Fransico. As well as having grown up in New York, I am aware that my own community rarely gets coverage because of the New York City focus.

The decision to make this a multimedia project was based on the skills I learned throughout my studies. This is a capstone project, and so I wanted to highlight and showcase my expertise in creating an engaging narrative story and converting that into an audio story. As well as showcasing my knowledge in web development and integrating UX and responsive web design.
